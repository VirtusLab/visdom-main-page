# Before / After

## Overall shift

- Before: the page presented Visdom as an `AI-Native SDLC Platform` with product hero stats, platform language, and product-style CTAs.
- After: the page presents VirtusLab as the team that embeds with enterprise engineering organizations to build agent-ready SDLC using Visdom components as the delivery kit.

## Section mapping

### 1. Hero

- Before: `Visdom 2.0` product hero with platform tagline, outcome stats, and `Explore the Platform`.
- After: forward-deployed hero with VirtusLab-led framing, Matrix assessment CTA, engagement CTA, and a capability-transfer visual.

### 2. Problem framing

- Before: a metrics strip focused on performance outcomes.
- After: a three-column observational section on the failure modes we see in enterprise AI adoption.

### 3. Differentiation

- Before: no explicit statement of the business model beyond platform positioning.
- After: a dedicated section explaining the in-between model: open-source kit, embedded delivery, and capability transfer.

### 4. Engagement model

- Before: no phased delivery model on the page.
- After: a three-phase engagement section with Foundation, Pilot Implementation, and Scale and Transfer.

### 5. Component section

- Before: `Platform Components` / `Building Blocks of Agent-Native SDLC` with outcome-heavy marketing copy for each item.
- After: `The kit we bring` with each component rewritten into a technical purpose, `What it does`, and `How we deploy it`.

### 6. Thesis section

- Before: article series were framed as thought leadership below the product story.
- After: the Matrix methodology, public writing, and article series are combined into a thesis section that explains how VirtusLab thinks and works.

### 7. AI Radar

- Before: packaged Radar section with generic ecosystem framing.
- After: custom Radar section that explains why clients use it and keeps the existing live stats plus dashboard link.

### 8. Final CTA

- Before: three equal-weight cards for trying ViDIA, taking the assessment, or talking to engineers.
- After: an asymmetric `Start the conversation` section with the Matrix assessment as the primary path, plus scoping call and open-source entry point.

## Supporting changes

- Updated nav anchors to point to `#kit`, `#thesis`, `#radar`, and `#start`.
- Updated page metadata to match the new forward-deployed positioning.
- Removed product-led hero claims from the top of the page.
